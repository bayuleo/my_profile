# Content Research Guideline

## Purpose

Produce recruiter-facing profile content that is accurate, concise, and supported by evidence. Treat every claim as unverified until it can be traced to an authoritative source or explicitly confirmed by the profile owner.

## Source priority

Use sources in this order:

1. Profile owner-provided facts, CV, portfolio notes, or written answers.
2. The owner's public repositories, project documentation, and shipped work.
3. Public professional profiles controlled by the owner.
4. Employer, client, conference, publication, or certification pages.
5. Search results and third-party summaries only as leads, never as sole evidence.

Record the source name and URL for every material claim. Note the access date when the source may change.

## Research workflow

1. Define the content need: headline, summary, experience, project, skills, education, certification, or achievement.
2. Collect candidate facts from the highest-priority available sources.
3. Separate facts from interpretation. Facts include dates, roles, technologies, deliverables, and measurable outcomes. Interpretation includes adjectives such as “expert,” “senior,” or “high impact.”
4. Verify each fact against at least one authoritative source; verify high-risk claims against two independent sources when practical.
5. Ask the profile owner to confirm any missing dates, ambiguous role scope, private work, metrics, or claims that cannot be independently verified.
6. Draft concise copy using only verified facts and clearly labeled owner-provided information.
7. Run the validation checklist before publishing.

## Evidence record

Maintain a compact evidence table for each profile section:

| Claim | Source | Evidence | Confidence | Follow-up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Example claim | Source title and URL | Exact supporting detail or location | High / Medium / Low | Question or `None` |

Do not include unsupported claims in the final copy. If evidence is incomplete, use neutral wording or mark the item as requiring confirmation.

## Writing rules

- Prefer specific responsibilities and outcomes over generic praise.
- Preserve the owner's actual job title and chronology unless the owner approves an editorial equivalent.
- Name technologies only when the source shows meaningful use of them.
- Include metrics only when the measurement, timeframe, and scope are known.
- Do not infer employment, client relationships, leadership scope, seniority, revenue impact, or production status.
- Do not expose private contact details, credentials, secrets, or confidential project information.
- Use clear recruiter-facing language, but do not optimize wording by overstating experience.
- Keep uncertainty visible in research notes and resolve it before publication.

## Validation checklist

- [ ] Every material claim has a recorded source.
- [ ] Dates, role names, organizations, and project names are consistent across sources.
- [ ] Metrics include enough context to avoid misleading interpretation.
- [ ] No skill is listed solely because it appears in a tool, dependency file, or keyword list.
- [ ] No confidential or personally sensitive information is included.
- [ ] Unverified claims are removed or explicitly sent to the owner for confirmation.
- [ ] Final copy is concise, readable, and faithful to the evidence.

## Deliverable format

For each researched section, provide:

1. Proposed copy.
2. Evidence table or source list.
3. Open questions and unverifiable claims.
4. A confidence rating: `High`, `Medium`, or `Low`.

The section is ready for review only when all open questions are either resolved or clearly called out to the profile owner.
